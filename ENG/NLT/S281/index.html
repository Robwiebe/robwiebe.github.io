The Narrator

101 Then Jesus left Capernaum and went down to the region of Judea and into the area east of the Jordan River. Once again crowds gathered around him, and as usual he was teaching them. 2 Some Pharisees came and tried to trap him with this question:

The Pharisees

“Should a man be allowed to divorce his wife?”

The Narrator

3 Jesus answered them with a question:

Jesus

“What did Moses say in the law about divorce?”

The Pharisees

4 “Well, he permitted it,”

The Narrator

they replied.

The Pharisees

“He said a man can give his wife a written notice of divorce and send her away.”*

The Narrator

5 But Jesus responded,

Jesus

“He wrote this commandment only as a concession to your hard hearts. 6 But ‘God made them male and female’* from the beginning of creation. 7 ‘This explains why a man leaves his father and mother and is joined to his wife,* 8 and the two are united into one.’* Since they are no longer two but one, 9 let no one split apart what God has joined together.”

The Narrator

10 Later, when he was alone with his disciples in the house, they brought up the subject again. 11 He told them,

Jesus

“Whoever divorces his wife and marries someone else commits adultery against her. 12 And if a woman divorces her husband and marries someone else, she commits adultery.”

The Narrator

13 One day some parents brought their children to Jesus so he could touch and bless them. But the disciples scolded the parents for bothering him. 14 When Jesus saw what was happening, he was angry with his disciples. He said to them,

Jesus

“Let the children come to me. Don’t stop them! For the Kingdom of God belongs to those who are like these children. 15 I tell you the truth, anyone who doesn’t receive the Kingdom of God like a child will never enter it.”

The Narrator

16 Then he took the children in his arms and placed his hands on their heads and blessed them. 17 As Jesus was starting out on his way to Jerusalem, a man came running up to him, knelt down, and asked,

A Rich Young Man

“Good Teacher, what must I do to inherit eternal life?”

Jesus

18 “Why do you call me good?”

The Narrator

Jesus asked.

Jesus

“Only God is truly good. 19 But to answer your question, you know the commandments: ‘You must not murder. You must not commit adultery. You must not steal. You must not testify falsely. You must not cheat anyone. Honor your father and mother.’*”

A Rich Young Man

20 “Teacher,”

The Narrator

the man replied,

A Rich Young Man

“I’ve obeyed all these commandments since I was young.”

The Narrator

21 Looking at the man, Jesus felt genuine love for him.

Jesus

“There is still one thing you haven’t done,”

The Narrator

he told him.

Jesus

“Go and sell all your possessions and give the money to the poor, and you will have treasure in heaven. Then come, follow me.”

The Narrator

22 At this the man’s face fell, and he went away sad, for he had many possessions. 23 Jesus looked around and said to his disciples,

Jesus

“How hard it is for the rich to enter the Kingdom of God!”

The Narrator

24 This amazed them. But Jesus said again,

Jesus

“Dear children, it is very hard* to enter the Kingdom of God. 25 In fact, it is easier for a camel to go through the eye of a needle than for a rich person to enter the Kingdom of God!”

The Narrator

26 The disciples were astounded.

The Disciples

“Then who in the world can be saved?”

The Narrator

they asked. 27 Jesus looked at them intently and said,

Jesus

“Humanly speaking, it is impossible. But not with God. Everything is possible with God.”

The Narrator

28 Then Peter began to speak up.

Simon Peter

“We’ve given up everything to follow you,”

The Narrator

he said.

Jesus

29 “Yes,”

The Narrator

Jesus replied,

Jesus

“and I assure you that everyone who has given up house or brothers or sisters or mother or father or children or property, for my sake and for the Good News, 30 will receive now in return a hundred times as many houses, brothers, sisters, mothers, children, and property—along with persecution. And in the world to come that person will have eternal life. 31 But many who are the greatest now will be least important then, and those who seem least important now will be the greatest then.*”

The Narrator

32 They were now on the way up to Jerusalem, and Jesus was walking ahead of them. The disciples were filled with awe, and the people following behind were overwhelmed with fear. Taking the twelve disciples aside, Jesus once more began to describe everything that was about to happen to him.

Jesus

33 “Listen,”

The Narrator

he said,

Jesus

“we’re going up to Jerusalem, where the Son of Man* will be betrayed to the leading priests and the teachers of religious law. They will sentence him to die and hand him over to the Romans.* 34 They will mock him, spit on him, flog him with a whip, and kill him, but after three days he will rise again.”

The Narrator

35 Then James and John, the sons of Zebedee, came over and spoke to him.

James the Disciple & John the Disciple

“Teacher,”

The Narrator

they said,

James the Disciple & John the Disciple

“we want you to do us a favor.”

Jesus

36 “What is your request?”

The Narrator

he asked. 37 They replied,

James the Disciple & John the Disciple

“When you sit on your glorious throne, we want to sit in places of honor next to you, one on your right and the other on your left.”

The Narrator

38 But Jesus said to them,

Jesus

“You don’t know what you are asking! Are you able to drink from the bitter cup of suffering I am about to drink? Are you able to be baptized with the baptism of suffering I must be baptized with?”

James the Disciple & John the Disciple

39 “Oh yes,”

The Narrator

they replied,

James the Disciple & John the Disciple

“we are able!”

The Narrator

Then Jesus told them,

Jesus

“You will indeed drink from my bitter cup and be baptized with my baptism of suffering. 40 But I have no right to say who will sit on my right or my left. God has prepared those places for the ones he has chosen.”

The Narrator

41 When the ten other disciples heard what James and John had asked, they were indignant. 42 So Jesus called them together and said,

Jesus

“You know that the rulers in this world lord it over their people, and officials flaunt their authority over those under them. 43 But among you it will be different. Whoever wants to be a leader among you must be your servant, 44 and whoever wants to be first among you must be the slave of everyone else. 45 For even the Son of Man came not to be served but to serve others and to give his life as a ransom for many.”

The Narrator

46 Then they reached Jericho, and as Jesus and his disciples left town, a large crowd followed him. A blind beggar named Bartimaeus (son of Timaeus) was sitting beside the road. 47 When Bartimaeus heard that Jesus of Nazareth was nearby, he began to shout,

Blind Bartimaeus

“Jesus, Son of David, have mercy on me!”

Some People

48 “Be quiet!”

The Narrator

many of the people yelled at him. But he only shouted louder,

Blind Bartimaeus

“Son of David, have mercy on me!”

The Narrator

49 When Jesus heard him, he stopped and said,

Jesus

“Tell him to come here.”

The Narrator

So they called the blind man.

Some People

“Cheer up,”

The Narrator

they said.

Some People

“Come on, he’s calling you!”

The Narrator

50 Bartimaeus threw aside his coat, jumped up, and came to Jesus.

Jesus

51 “What do you want me to do for you?”

The Narrator

Jesus asked.

Blind Bartimaeus

“My rabbi,*”

The Narrator

the blind man said,

Blind Bartimaeus

“I want to see!”

The Narrator

52 And Jesus said to him,

Jesus

“Go, for your faith has healed you.”

The Narrator

Instantly the man could see, and he followed Jesus down the road.* 111 As Jesus and his disciples approached Jerusalem, they came to the towns of Bethphage and Bethany on the Mount of Olives. Jesus sent two of them on ahead.

Jesus

2 “Go into that village over there,”

The Narrator

he told them.

Jesus

“As soon as you enter it, you will see a young donkey tied there that no one has ever ridden. Untie it and bring it here. 3 If anyone asks, ‘What are you doing?’ just say, ‘The Lord needs it and will return it soon.’”

The Narrator

4 The two disciples left and found the colt standing in the street, tied outside the front door. 5 As they were untying it, some bystanders demanded,

Some People

“What are you doing, untying that colt?”

The Narrator

6 They said what Jesus had told them to say, and they were permitted to take it. 7 Then they brought the colt to Jesus and threw their garments over it, and he sat on it. 8 Many in the crowd spread their garments on the road ahead of him, and others spread leafy branches they had cut in the fields. 9 Jesus was in the center of the procession, and the people all around him were shouting,

The Crowd

“Praise God!* Blessings on the one who comes in the name of the Lord! 10 Blessings on the coming Kingdom of our ancestor David! Praise God in highest heaven!”*

The Narrator

11 So Jesus came to Jerusalem and went into the Temple. After looking around carefully at everything, he left because it was late in the afternoon. Then he returned to Bethany with the twelve disciples. 12 The next morning as they were leaving Bethany, Jesus was hungry. 13 He noticed a fig tree in full leaf a little way off, so he went over to see if he could find any figs. But there were only leaves because it was too early in the season for fruit. 14 Then Jesus said to the tree,

Jesus

“May no one ever eat your fruit again!”

The Narrator

And the disciples heard him say it. 15 When they arrived back in Jerusalem, Jesus entered the Temple and began to drive out the people buying and selling animals for sacrifices. He knocked over the tables of the money changers and the chairs of those selling doves, 16 and he stopped everyone from using the Temple as a marketplace.* 17 He said to them,

Jesus

“The Scriptures declare, ‘My Temple will be called a house of prayer for all nations,’ but you have turned it into a den of thieves.”*

The Narrator

18 When the leading priests and teachers of religious law heard what Jesus had done, they began planning how to kill him. But they were afraid of him because the people were so amazed at his teaching. 19 That evening Jesus and the disciples left* the city. 20 The next morning as they passed by the fig tree he had cursed, the disciples noticed it had withered from the roots up. 21 Peter remembered what Jesus had said to the tree on the previous day and exclaimed,

Simon Peter

“Look, Rabbi! The fig tree you cursed has withered and died!”

The Narrator

22 Then Jesus said to the disciples,

Jesus

“Have faith in God. 23 I tell you the truth, you can say to this mountain, ‘May you be lifted up and thrown into the sea,’ and it will happen. But you must really believe it will happen and have no doubt in your heart. 24 I tell you, you can pray for anything, and if you believe that you’ve received it, it will be yours. 25 But when you are praying, first forgive anyone you are holding a grudge against, so that your Father in heaven will forgive your sins, too.*”

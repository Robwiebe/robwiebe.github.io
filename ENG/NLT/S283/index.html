3 Meanwhile, Jesus was in Bethany at the home of Simon, a man who had previously had leprosy. While he was eating,* a woman came in with a beautiful alabaster jar of expensive perfume made from essence of nard. She broke open the jar and poured the perfume over his head. 4 Some of those at the table were indignant.

Some Dinner Guests

“Why waste such expensive perfume?”

The Narrator

they asked.

Some Dinner Guests

5 “It could have been sold for a year’s wages* and the money given to the poor!”

The Narrator

So they scolded her harshly. 6 But Jesus replied,

Jesus

“Leave her alone. Why criticize her for doing such a good thing to me? 7 You will always have the poor among you, and you can help them whenever you want to. But you will not always have me. 8 She has done what she could and has anointed my body for burial ahead of time. 9 I tell you the truth, wherever the Good News is preached throughout the world, this woman’s deed will be remembered and discussed.”

The Narrator

10 Then Judas Iscariot, one of the twelve disciples, went to the leading priests to arrange to betray Jesus to them. 11 They were delighted when they heard why he had come, and they promised to give him money. So he began looking for an opportunity to betray Jesus. 12 On the first day of the Festival of Unleavened Bread, when the Passover lamb is sacrificed, Jesus’ disciples asked him,

The Disciples

“Where do you want us to go to prepare the Passover meal for you?”

The Narrator

13 So Jesus sent two of them into Jerusalem with these instructions:

Jesus

“As you go into the city, a man carrying a pitcher of water will meet you. Follow him. 14 At the house he enters, say to the owner, ‘The Teacher asks: Where is the guest room where I can eat the Passover meal with my disciples?’ 15 He will take you upstairs to a large room that is already set up. That is where you should prepare our meal.”

The Narrator

16 So the two disciples went into the city and found everything just as Jesus had said, and they prepared the Passover meal there. 17 In the evening Jesus arrived with the twelve disciples.* 18 As they were at the table* eating, Jesus said,

Jesus

“I tell you the truth, one of you eating with me here will betray me.”

The Narrator

19 Greatly distressed, each one asked in turn,

The Disciples

“Am I the one?”

The Narrator

20 He replied,

Jesus

“It is one of you twelve who is eating from this bowl with me. 21 For the Son of Man* must die, as the Scriptures declared long ago. But how terrible it will be for the one who betrays him. It would be far better for that man if he had never been born!”

The Narrator

22 As they were eating, Jesus took some bread and blessed it. Then he broke it in pieces and gave it to the disciples, saying,

Jesus

“Take it, for this is my body.”

The Narrator

Jesus

Jesus

Jesus

Jesus

“Go ahead and sleep. Have your rest. But no—the time has come. The Son of Man is betrayed into the hands of sinners. 42 Up, let’s be going. Look, my betrayer is here!”

“You will know which one to arrest when I greet him with a kiss. Then you can take him away under guard.”

“Rabbi!”

Jesus

There he sat with the guards, warming himself by the fire. 55 Inside, the leading priests and the entire high council* were trying to find evidence against Jesus, so they could put him to death.
